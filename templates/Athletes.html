Milkha Singh

Milkha Singh from Punjab, also known as "The Flying Sikh," was an Indian track and field athlete and one of the country's most legendary sports personalities. Milkha Singh specialized in middle-distance and sprint events, particularly the 400 meters race. He represented India in several international competitions, including the Olympic Games and Commonwealth Games, throughout the 1950s and 1960s. One of Milkha Singh's most notable achievements was at the 1958 Asian Games held in Tokyo, where he won multiple medals, including a gold medal in the 400 meters race, making him the first Indian athlete to achieve this feat at an international sporting event.

Dola Banerjee

A veteran of two Olympic Games, Dola Banerjee is India's first-ever Indian archer on an international level. She participated in Athens 2004, becoming the first Indian woman archer to qualify for the Olympic Games. Banerjee's list of “the firsts” continued when she won a Gold Medal at the 2007 Archery World Cup, the first Indian woman and second overall to do so. She also won the team gold at the 2010 Commonwealth Games and followed it with a bronze in the individual event. Yet another bronze came in the team event at the 2010 Asian Games.

Pusarla Venkata Sindhu (P.V. Sindhu)

Pusarla Venkata Sindhu (P.V. Sindhu) is from Telangana, she won the silver medal in women's singles badminton at the 2016 Summer Olympics held in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Her remarkable performance in the tournament earned her recognition as the first Indian woman to win a silver medal at the Olympics. At the 2020 Summer Olympics (held in 2021 due to postponement), Sindhu continued her impressive run and won the bronze medal in the women's singles event. With this achievement, she became the first Indian woman to win two Olympic medals.

Esow Alben

Esow Alben is an Indian track cyclist from Andaman and Nicobar Islands. He is the first Indian to win medal at the 2018 UCI Junior Track Cycling World Championships when he won Silver at Keirin event in Aigle, Switzerland. In same year he also won 3 Golds in Team Sprint, Keirin and 200m Time Trial at Junior Asian Track Cycling championships in Nilai, Malaysia. In 2019, Esow won 3 Golds in Sprint, Team Sprint and Keirin event at Junior Asian Track Cycling championships in Jakarta, Indonesia.

Major Dhyan Chand

Major Dhyan Chand was from Prayagraj, he was a key player in the Indian men's hockey team during the 1920s and 1930s, considered the golden era of Indian hockey. He won three Olympic gold medals with the Indian team at the Summer Olympics: 1928 (Amsterdam), 1932 (Los Angeles), and 1936 (Berlin). Dhyan Chand's goal-scoring abilities, extraordinary ball control, and incredible dribbling skills earned him the nickname "The Wizard" and "The Magician" of hockey.

Neeraj Chopra

Neeraj Chopra from Haryana is one of India's most promising athletes in javelin throw and has represented the country in various international competitions. He won the gold medal in javelin throw at the 2018 Asian Games held in Jakarta, Indonesia. His throw of 88.06 meters earned him the top spot on the podium and made him the first Indian javelin thrower to win an Asian Games gold medal. Neeraj Chopra has also achieved success in the Commonwealth Games, winning a gold medal at the 2018 Gold Coast Commonwealth Games with a throw of 86.47 meters.

Abhinav Bindra

Abhinav Bindra from Uttarakhand is India's most successful shooter and the country's only individual Olympic gold medalist in shooting. He won the gold medal in the 10m Air Rifle event at the 2008 Summer Olympics held in Beijing, China. His remarkable performance in the final round earned him the top spot on the podium, making him the first Indian to win an individual Olympic gold medal in any sport. Abhinav Bindra has also won multiple medals at the Commonwealth Games and Asian Games, showcasing his consistency and excellence in the sport.

Leander Paes

Leander Paes from West Bengal is one of India's most successful and celebrated tennis players in Olympic history. He has represented India in seven Olympic Games, starting from the 1992 Barcelona Olympics and continuing until the 2016 Rio de Janeiro Olympics. He became the first Indian to compete in seven consecutive Summer Olympics. Paes has won an Olympic medal for India. He achieved this feat at the 1996 Atlanta Olympics, where he won the bronze medal in men's singles tennis. In addition to his Olympic success, Leander Paes has an illustrious tennis career in doubles and mixed doubles. He has won numerous Grand Slam titles, including Wimbledon, the US Open, the Australian Open, and the French Open. Paes is known for his strong net play, excellent volleying skills, and remarkable achievements in doubles events.

Karnam Malleswari

Karnam Malleswari from Andhra Pradesh is India's most successful weightlifter in Olympic history. She won the bronze medal in the women's 69 kg category at the 2000 Summer Olympics held in Sydney, Australia. Her remarkable performance in the event earned her the distinction of being the first Indian woman to win an Olympic medal. Karnam Malleswari's bronze medal win at the 2000 Sydney Olympics was a historic moment for Indian weightlifting and women's sports. She showcased her strength, determination, and skill on the international stage, becoming an inspiration for aspiring weightlifters in India.

Sushil Kumar

Sushil Kumar from Delhi is one of India's most successful and celebrated wrestlers in Olympic history. He won a bronze medal in men's freestyle wrestling (66 kg category) at the 2008 Summer Olympics held in Beijing, China. This victory made him the first Indian wrestler to win an Olympic medal in 56 years. At the 2012 Summer Olympics held in London, United Kingdom, Sushil Kumar achieved a historic feat by winning a silver medal in the same weight category. He became the first Indian athlete to win back-to-back Olympic medals. In addition to his Olympic success, Sushil Kumar has won numerous medals in other international wrestling events, including the Commonwealth Games, Asian Games, and World Championships. Sushil Kumar's achievements have significantly contributed to popularizing wrestling in India and inspiring a new generation of wrestlers in the country.

Dipa Karmakar

Dipa Karmakar from Tripura is India's most accomplished and renowned gymnast in Olympic history. She represented India at the 2016 Rio de Janeiro Olympics, becoming the first Indian female gymnast to qualify for the Olympic Games. In the women's vault event, Dipa executed the difficult Produnova vault, which is considered one of the most challenging and risky vaults in gymnastics. Her daring performance in the finals earned her a fourth-place finish, narrowly missing the bronze medal. Dipa Karmakar's achievements in gymnastics have been groundbreaking for Indian gymnastics. Her successful execution of the Produnova vault brought international attention and acclaim to her skills and fearlessness as a gymnast.
